## The 90-Day Brand Building Blueprint

### Week 1-2: Foundation (What You Stand For)

**Most people skip this. Don't.**

**Exercise: Define Your Three Pillars**

Your brand should be known for 3 specific things.

**Bad (too vague):**
- "Technology"
- "Leadership"
- "Innovation"

**Good (specific):**
- "AI agent architecture for production systems"
- "Leading distributed engineering teams in fintech"
- "Zero-to-one product launches in B2B SaaS"

**Your turn:**
1. What are you deeply experienced in?
2. What do people ask your advice about?
3. What would you talk about for 3 hours without notes?

**Those are your three pillars.**

**My example:**
1. Product strategy for B2B SaaS
2. Hiring & building product teams
3. PMF validation frameworks

**Every piece of content you create should serve one of these three pillars.**

### Week 3-4: Platform Selection (Where to Show Up)

**Don't try to be everywhere. Pick 2 platforms.**

**Platform Matrix:**

| Platform | Best For | Time Investment | ROI for Brand |
|----------|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| LinkedIn | B2B, executives, job hunting | Medium | High |
| Twitter | Tech, builders, fast feedback | High | Medium-High |
| Medium | Long-form thought leadership | Low | Medium |
| YouTube | Deep tutorials, courses | Very High | High (long-term) |
| Newsletter | Owned audience | Medium | Very High |

**Recommended combos:**

**For career changers:**
- LinkedIn (primary) + Medium (secondary)

**For builders:**
- Twitter (primary) + Newsletter (secondary)

**For executives:**
- LinkedIn (primary) + Newsletter (secondary)

**Start with 1 primary. Add secondary after month 2.**

### Week 5-8: Content System (What to Share)

**The mistake:** Trying to be original every day.

**The reality:** You don't need original content. You need consistent, valuable content.

**The 60-30-10 Rule:**

**60% - Curated/Shared Content (easy):**
- Share others' content with your take
- "This is a great breakdown of X. What I'd add..."
- Takes 5 minutes

**30% - Personal Experience (medium):**
- Lessons from your work
- Mistakes you made
- Problems you solved
- Takes 15 minutes

**10% - Original Insights (hard):**
- Novel frameworks
- Deep analysis
- Thought leadership
- Takes 1-2 hours

**Example Content Calendar (LinkedIn):**

**Monday:** Share article on AI, add your take (5 min)
**Wednesday:** Story from your week (15 min)
**Friday:** Longer post on lesson learned (30 min)

**Output:** 3 posts/week, ~1 hour total.

### Week 9-12: Engagement System (How to Build Network)

**Most people post and ghost. That doesn't work.**

**The Engagement Formula:**

**Before you post (15 min):**
- Comment thoughtfully on 3 posts in your niche
- Not "Great post!" → "This resonates. I've found X approach works when Y."

**After you post (30 min):**
- Respond to every comment (first 2 hours critical)
- Ask questions to continue conversation
- DM interesting commenters to connect

**Daily routine:**
- 15 min: Engage on others' content
- 15 min: Create/post your content
- 30 min: Engage on your content

**Total: 1 hour/day**

**Result:** Your network compounds exponentially.

## The Content Frameworks That Actually Work

### Framework 1: The "I Used to Think" Post

**Structure:**
```
I used to think [common belief].

Then [experience happened].

Now I think [new belief].

Here's why:
1. [Reason]
2. [Reason]
3. [Reason]
```

**Example:**
"I used to think product-market fit was binary. Then I launched 3 products. Now I know it's a spectrum. Here's why..."

**Why it works:** Shows evolution, vulnerability, insight.

### Framework 2: The "Here's What Nobody Tells You" Post

**Structure:**
```
Everyone says [conventional wisdom].

But here's what they don't tell you:

[Inconvenient truth]
[Inconvenient truth]
[Inconvenient truth]

What to do instead: [advice]
```

**Example:**
"Everyone says 'learn to code.' But here's what they don't tell you: 1) Syntax is easy, system design is hard. 2) Code is 20% of the job, communication is 50%. 3) Without domain knowledge, you're just a code monkey."

**Why it works:** Contrarian, but helpful.

### Framework 3: The "Detailed Breakdown" Post

**Structure:**
```
I analyzed [thing].

Here's what I found:

[Insight 1 + data/example]
[Insight 2 + data/example]
[Insight 3 + data/example]

Takeaway: [so what?]
```

**Example:**
"I analyzed 50 successful career transitions. Here's what every one had in common: 1) Built in public (shared progress). 2) Networked before applying (warm intros). 3) Had portfolio (proved ability)."

**Why it works:** Data-driven, actionable.

## The Fast-Track: "Build in Public" Strategy

**This is the cheat code.**

**Instead of just posting:** Document what you're building.

**If you're changing careers:**

**Week 1:** "I'm transitioning from marketing to product management. Here's my 90-day plan."

**Week 3:** "Day 14 of learning product management. Today I built my first product spec. Here's what I learned..."

**Week 6:** "Just shipped my first side project. Here's what went wrong (and right)."

**Result:**
- You're building credibility in real-time
- People root for you (underdog story)
- Network grows organically
- When you're ready to apply, you have proof

## Common Mistakes (And How to Avoid)

### Mistake 1: "I'll Post When I Have Something Important to Say"

**Problem:** You never will. Perfectionism kills momentum.

**Fix:** Lower the bar. Share observations, not just conclusions.

### Mistake 2: "Nobody's Engaging, Why Bother?"

**Problem:** It takes 3-6 months to build momentum.

**Fix:** Focus on consistency, not early metrics.

### Mistake 3: "I Don't Want to Be 'That Person' Who Posts Too Much"

**Problem:** You're invisible. Being "that person" is the goal.

**Fix:** Post 5-7x/week. Nobody unfollows for value.

### Mistake 4: "I'm Not an Expert"

**Problem:** You don't need to be. You need to be helpful.

**Fix:** Share what you're learning, not what you've mastered.
