# Why Your Career Intelligence System is Useless (And How to Build One That Works)

Your company spends $15K/year on learning platforms. Your team spends 2 hours/week in career conversations. And somehow, people are still blindsided by salary expectations, role clarity, and growth paths.

The problem isn't that you need more data. It's that you don't know what to do with the data you have.

## The Career Intelligence Gap

Most companies measure career metrics backward.

They wait until someone quits to realize they were underpaid. They discover retention problems in exit interviews. They find skill gaps when someone can't deliver.

The teams that retain talent do it differently. They know.

They know:
- Which roles are at retention risk (before the person knows)
- What comparable market salaries are (in real-time)
- What skills your team needs (before hiring)
- Where career stagnation is happening (when intervention still matters)

That's career intelligence. And it changes everything.

## Three Data Sources You're Probably Ignoring

**1. Internal Compensation Data**
You have it. You're terrified to look at it. The gap between highest and lowest earner for the same role? Probably 40-60%. The fact that you didn't realize it? That's your problem.

**2. Market Benchmarks**
Levels.fyi. Salary.com. LinkedIn. This data is public. Most companies ignore it. Smart ones baseline quarterly and flag anomalies.

**3. Skill-to-Career Mapping**
What skills predict promotions? What roles have highest retention? Which teams have fastest growth? This data is hiding in your HRIS. Extract it.

## The Three Metrics That Matter

**Retention Risk Score**
Combines: tenure, performance reviews, salary competitiveness, role satisfaction indicators.

Teams that track this retain 85-90% of high performers. Teams that don't? 60-70%.

**Career Velocity**
Measures: promotion timeline, skill development rate, compensation growth. Lets you identify who's stuck.

Early intervention: career conversation, skill training, lateral moves. Result: 70% of "stuck" employees re-engage.

**Market Competitiveness Index**
Shows: how your compensation stacks against market for each role. Red flags go straight to leadership.

Implementation: quarterly update. Five-minute review. Preventive adjustments cost 3x less than replacement hiring.

## What Most Companies Get Wrong

**Mistake 1: Only Looking at Salary**
Retention is 40% compensation, 60% career growth + autonomy + team. You need all three signals.

**Mistake 2: Annual Reviews Too Late**
By the time your annual review rolls around, the decision to leave was made months ago. Quarterly is minimum. Monthly is better.

**Mistake 3: Treating It as HR's Problem**
Retention is a CEO-level problem. It affects unit economics, product velocity, culture. Treat it that way.

## Three Actions This Week

1. **Pull your compensation data.** Literally run a query. How much variation is there for same roles? Is it justified?

2. **Check market benchmarks.** Grab data from Levels.fyi for your locations and roles. How do you stack up?

3. **Talk to your team.** Not about compensation. About career. Where do they see themselves in 2 years? What's blocking them?
